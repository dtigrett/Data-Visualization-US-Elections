    Correlations Discovered Between Voter turnout and Income

    • The charts above display negative correlations between Voter Turnout 2016 and Voter Turnout 2020 in relation to poverty and household income. The visible trend indicates that less affluent and poorer states have -0.44 r-value correlation for 2016 and -0.55 r-value correlation for 2020. Hence, Poorer states always had higher rates of Voter Turnouts. There is positive correlation of 0.39 r-value between Voter Turnout 2020 and household income. There is positive correlation of 0.15 r-value between Voter Turnout 2016 and household income.


    Correlations Discovered Between Voter turnout and Age

    • The correlations between Voter Turnout and age are are weekly positively related. The median ages in most US states are between 36-40 years old. There is positive correlation of 0.32 r-value between Voter Turnout 2020 and median age. There is positive correlation of 0.40 r-value between Voter Turnout 2016 and median age.
